Chapter 2: Vectors and Matrices

Basic Vector Operations

The dot product

MATH(A \cdot B = A_1B_1 + A_2B_2 + ...)
MATH(A \cdot B = \left \| A \right \| \cdot \left \| B \right \| \cdot \cos \angle{AB})

The cross product

INLINE(A \times B) is only defined for 3 dimensional vectors. It produces a vector normal to the plane defined by its operands, with the magnitude defined by the product of their magnitudes and the sin of the angle between them. INLINE((\left \| A \right \| \cdot \left \| B \right \| \cdot \sin \angle{AB})) The elegant way to write the cross product is:

MATH(A \times B = \begin{vmatrix} \textup{i} & \textup{j} & \textup{k} \\ A_1 & A_2 & A_3 \\ B_1 & B_2 & B_3 \end{vmatrix})

Normal (unit length) vectors are often denoted INLINE(\hat v)

MATH(\hat v = \frac{v}{\left \| v \right \|})
