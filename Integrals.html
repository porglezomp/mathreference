Chapter 3: Integrals

Line integrals

Line integrals are useful for finding the effect of a function along a certain path.
INLINE(\int_C f(t)\ dt) will give the length of the path C.
For a line integral, you will need:

MATH(x = f(t), y = f(t), \frac{dx}{dt}=f'(t), \frac{dy}{dt}=g'(t)) MATH(\frac{ds}{dt} = \sqrt{f'(t)^2 + g'(t)^2})

and then integrate INLINE(\int_C ds\ dt) to find the length.

The line integral for the length of a curve defined as f(t) and y(t) is

MATH(L=\int_a^b\sqrt{dx^2+dy^2}\ dt)
