Chapter 1: Trigonometric Identities

Pythorean Identities

MATH(\sin^2\theta+\cos^2\theta = 1)
MATH(1 + \tan^2\theta = \sec^2\theta)
MATH(1 + \cot^2\theta = \csc^2\theta)

Sum and Difference

MATH(\sin(u \pm v) = \sin u \cos v \pm \cos u \sin v)
MATH(\cos(u \pm v) = \cos y \cos v \mp \sin y \sin v)
MATH(\tan(u \pm v) = \frac{\tan u \pm \tan v}{1 \mp \tan u \tan v})

Double Angle Identities

MATH(\sin(2\theta) = 2 \sin \theta \cos \theta)
MATH(\cos(2\theta) = \cos^2\theta - \sin^2\theta)
MATH(\tan(2\theta) = \frac{2\tan\theta}{1 - \tan^2\theta})

Half-Angle Identities

MATH(\sin{\frac{x}{2}} = \pm \sqrt{\frac{1 - \cos x}{2})
MATH(\cos{\frac{x}{2}} = \pm \sqrt{\frac{1 + \cos x}{x})
MATH(\tan{\frac{x}{2}} = \pm \sqrt{\frac{1 - \cos x}{1 + \cos x}} = \frac{1 - \cos x}{\sin x} = \frac{\sin x}{1 + \cos x})
These can be alternatively written for the square of the operation:
MATH(\sin^2 x = \frac{1 - \cos{2x}}{2})
MATH(\cos^2 x = \frac{1 + \cos{2x}}{2})
MATH(\tan^2 x = \frac{1 - \cos{2x}}{1 + \cos{2x}})

Product Identities

MATH(\sin(x) \cos(y) = \frac{\sin(x + y) + \sin(x - y)}{2})
MATH(\cos(x) \sin(y) = \frac{\sin(x + y) - \sin(x - y)}{2})
MATH(\cos(x) \cos(y) = \frac{\cos(x - y) + \cos(x + y)}{2})
MATH(\sin(x) \sin(y) = \frac{\cos(x - y) - \cos(x + y)}{2})

Periods

The period of sin, cos, sec, and csc is 2π.
The period of tan and cot is π.
