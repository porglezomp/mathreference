Chapter 3: Derivatives

The derivative of a function represents the rate of change of the function. Geometrically you can think of it as the slope, or the velocity of something in motion, or many other interpretations.

The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus

MATH(\frac{d}{dx} = \frac{f(x + \Delta x) - f(x)}{\Delta x})

Derivative Shortcuts

Basic rules

MATH((f + v)' = f' + g')
MATH((uv)' = f'g + g'f )
MATH(\left \( \frac{f}{g} \right \)' = \frac{f'g - g'f}{f^2})
MATH((f \circ g)'(x) = f'(g(x))g'(x))

Polynomials

MATH(\frac{d}{dx} mx^n = mnx^{n-1})
